DocSeek — Test 8 feedback survey (3 questions)
User story: As a user who already matched with a physician, I want to move directly to appointment scheduling so that I do not need to search for that doctor again on UPMC’s website.

This survey checks whether that story feels “done” enough that someone would pay for the product. Questions avoid gimmicky recommendation scores that invite sarcasm instead of truth. Framing draws on *The Lean Startup* (validated learning and outcomes, not vanity activity), *The Mom Test* (concrete past behavior and specifics), and common product metrics (time saved, error reduction, willingness to repeat).

---

Three salient metrics (what we are trying to learn)

1) Handoff success — Did the user reach the right provider context in one step?
   - Why: The story’s outcome is “no second search on UPMC.” If the link lands on the wrong person or a generic page, the feature fails regardless of pretty UI. This is a crisp outcome metric (similar to activation or conversion in lean terms), not a count of clicks inside DocSeek.

2) Time and cognitive load vs. the old way — How much hunting did they avoid?
   - Why: Willingness to pay tracks with replacing a painful workflow (directory search, spelling names, filtering). This maps to “job to be done” completion and retention: if it still feels like two products duct-taped together, value collapses.

3) Repeat use when returning — Would they trust this path when stressed or busy?
   - Why: Lecture-style engagement is meaningful when it predicts retention: users who would use **Saved physicians → Book appointment** after a first match are evidence the loop works in real life, not only in a demo.

---

Survey (answer in a few sentences each)

Question 1 — Behavior, not slogans
Last time you tried to book with a specific doctor through a health system website, what did you have to do first (search bar, filters, phone tree)? Compared to that, what happened when you used DocSeek’s **Book appointment**—did it feel like you skipped a whole chapter, or did you still end up doing the old steps anyway?

Question 2 — Correct-doctor anxiety without asking “Was our link good?”
When the new tab opened, what was the first thing you checked to be sure you were still looking at the same human being—not the hospital brand page, not a namesake? Did anything make you doubt you were in the right place?

Question 3 — Money talk without NPS
If DocSeek charged a small monthly fee on top of what you already pay for care, what would **Book appointment** (or the saved-doctor path) have to guarantee for you to shrug and pay—accurate profile, fewer dead clicks, something else? If you would never pay, what would have to change first?
