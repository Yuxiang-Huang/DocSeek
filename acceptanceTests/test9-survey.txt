DocSeek — Test 9 feedback survey (3 questions)
User story: As a patient experiencing potentially life-threatening symptoms, I want to be alerted if my condition requires immediate help, so I know to seek immediate care.

Below are three metrics we care about (Lean Startup: clear outcomes; Mom Test: concrete behavior; lecture-style: safety and trust, not vanity usage). Then three **short** questions—no “rate us 1–5” prompts.

---

Three salient metrics

1) Noticeability — Did the user see the warning without hunting for it?
   - Why: If the alert blends in, the story fails. This is an outcome metric: correct response only matters if the signal is perceived.

2) Comprehension — Did they know what to do next (emergency care vs. keep browsing doctors)?
   - Why: Willingness to pay for a health app tracks with trust that it won’t quietly steer people the wrong way in scary moments.

3) Trust / tone — Did the message feel responsible, not gimmicky or dismissive?
   - Why: Over-hype or vague copy erodes retention; calm clarity supports payment and continued use.

---

Survey (keep each answer to a sentence or two)

1) In one glance, was the emergency message easy to spot on the page, or easy to miss?

2) After reading it, what would you do next in real life—call emergency services, keep using the app, or something else?

3) Did the wording feel serious and helpful, or annoying? Why?
