DocSeek — Test 10 feedback survey (3 questions)
User story: As a patient exploring options, I want to save physicians I am interested in so that I can revisit them later without repeating my search.

Three metrics (Lean Startup: outcomes over vanity; Mom Test: real behavior; lecture-style: retention and “job done”)

1) Save-and-return success — Could they find a saved doctor again without redoing the search?
   - Why: This is the story’s core outcome. Clicks inside the app matter only if the loop closes.

2) Persistence confidence — After closing the tab or coming back tomorrow, is the list still there?
   - Why: Willingness to pay tracks with trust that work isn’t lost; this maps to retention and perceived reliability.

3) Effort vs. workarounds — Is this easier than screenshots, spreadsheets, or open browser tabs?
   - Why: If it isn’t clearly better than free habits, paid conversion rarely follows.

---

Survey (keep answers short)

1) Last time you shortlisted doctors outside this app, what did you use—notes app, screenshots, bookmarks? Was saving inside DocSeek simpler or not really?

2) After you saved someone, did you trust they’d still be there when you came back, or did you worry you’d have to search again?

3) If DocSeek cost money, what would “saved physicians” have to do for you to pay—sync across devices, reminders, something else, or would free tools always be enough?
